Reflections on our intervention

Show don't tell

Our decision to allow someone else to interact with our project rather than walking through how it worked ourselves was important to the project. It allowed the class to discover the ideas behind the project. Watching Jono interact with the piece was great and he reacted in ways we expected such as being frustrated with the loading screens and surprised at some of the tasks.

The small touches and performance

The performance of the presentation was crucial to some of its success. For example, during our presentation were asked if the web cam was actually doing anything. We responded that it was, despite the fact it did nothing.

The small touches of the intervention such as web cam are what really pulled everything together. Other things in this vein were the loading screens and animations on the drawer LEDs.

_Jono makes an audible sigh during this interaction with the loading screen._

A Blunder

Our decision to use Access Canberra logo and name as our fictional scenario was mistake, it changed the focus of the project from exploring questionable machine learning and exploitation of human attention. Instead people focused on the satire of a trip to Access Canberra which was instead supposed to represent a generic everyday place you visit. This is biggest change I would make to the design if we were going to present it again. I'm not sure how it should be staged to avoid this satirical element. Although, it probably needs to be placed in a situation where it isn't questioned by the people interacting with it. Which is precisely the the opposite of situation it was it.

Other Interventions

The other group's interventions were interesting, especially the group looking at fast fashion. The laughter was especially evocative.
